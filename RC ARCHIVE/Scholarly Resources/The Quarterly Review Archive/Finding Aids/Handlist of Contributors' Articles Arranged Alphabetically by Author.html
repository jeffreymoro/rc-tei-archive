Handlist of Contributors' Articles

Arranged Alphabetically by Author

In the handlist, under each author's name are articles attributed to him in the Index pages on this website. The list summarizes the results of original research identifying the authors of unsigned contributions to the Quarterly Review. The list covers the period 1809-24, the tenure of the journal's first editor, William Gifford. As pointed out elsewhere on this website, the primary contributors to the effort of identifying contributors to the early Quarterly Review are John Murray III, Hill Shine and Helen Chadwick Shine, and the present writer. (For information on the relative contribution of each of these researchers, the interested reader may consult An Introduction to this Website's Features.)

In the handlist the numbers represent article serial numbers assigned to Quarterly Review articles in Hill Shine and Helen Chadwick Shine, The Quarterly Review Under Gifford (1949). The abbreviation 'collab.' indicates that the contributor co-authored the article with another writer. The abbreviation 'prob.' indicates that the attribution, while well founded, is less than certain. Article serial numbers following 'See' or 'See also' indicate a) articles for which some but not strong evidence of attribution has been found for this author, or b) the author contributed material or sub-edited the article but was not a co-author.
