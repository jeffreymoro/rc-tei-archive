3129. Robert Southey to John Rickman, 30 April [1818]

Endorsement: 30 April – 19
MS: Huntington Library, RS 367. ALS; 3p.
Previously published: John Wood Warter (ed.), Selections from the Letters of Robert Southey, 4 vols (London, 1856), III, p. 129 [misdated ‘1819’].
Dating note: Year from letter content.

My dear R.

The chest of oranges occasioned a great deal of speculation in this family, there was no indication whence it came, farther than that Liverpool was the place, but to whose good works it was to be set down we could not devise. I wish Mrs R. had seen the children when it arrived; – I was expecting a box of books from Milan, they called me down with news that it was come, which to them was a great joy, but their astonishment when they discovered the contents would have made no bad subject for a picture. The oranges were very good, & part of them are still existing in the form of marmalade, the first specimen of my daughter Ediths manufactory in this kind.

A table of weights & measures at the end of the book will save me the trouble of frequent calculations.

(1)
‘Table of Weights, Measures, and Money’, History of Brazil, 3 vols (London, 1810–1819), III, p. 900.
I never mean to use a foreign appellation in the text, unless it has been previously explained, & has no equivalent term in English, – which must very often be the case.

I am surprized at the delay of the Review.

(2)
Quarterly Review, 18 (January 1818), published 9 June 1818.

General Crauford is going to send me the few papers which remain of his brothers who was killed at Ciudad Rodrigo; – for the greater part were lost with his effects after his death.

(3)
Major-General Robert Craufurd (1764–1812; DNB). Southey commemorated his death on 23 January 1812 in his ‘For the Walls of Ciudad Rodrigo’, one of a series of Inscriptions on the Peninsular War published in his Poetical Works, 10 vols (London, 1837–1838), III, pp. 150–151.

God bless you
RS.

30 April.
