3471. Robert Southey to the President and Council of the Royal Academy, 26 April [1820]

MS: Royal Academy of Arts, London, FAR/2/142. L; 1p.
Unpublished.
Dating note: This unsigned note is in another hand, probably Henry Herbert Southey’s, and was written at a time when Robert Southey expected to arrive in London in late April 1820. He was delayed, and did not arrive in town until 1 May 1820, thus missing the annual dinner of the Royal Academy of Arts on 29 April.

Mr Southey will do himself the Honor of dining with the President & Council of the Royal Academy on Saturday the 29th Inst

(1)
The date, in 1820, of the Annual Dinner of the Royal Academy. The event had been inaugurated on 21 April 1771 by Sir Joshua Reynolds (1723–1792; DNB), the Academy’s first president. Southey was, in the end, unable to attend as his arrival in London was delayed.
