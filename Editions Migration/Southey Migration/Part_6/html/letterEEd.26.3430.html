3430. Robert Southey to John Rickman, 2 February 1820

Endorsement: Fr RS./ 2 Feby 1820
MS: Huntington Library, RS 387. ALS; 2p.
Unpublished.

My dear R.

Dr S. has taken it in his head that I am a very proper person to –– write a hymn for the Humane Society!

(1)
The Royal Humane Society, founded 1774 to promote lifesaving.
– an honour which I have begged leave to decline.

So we have seen the end of a reign of sixty years, the most eventful in history!

(2)
George III (1738–1820; King of Great Britain 1760–1820; DNB) died on 29 January 1820.
– What an ugly coincidence that the proclamation ought to have been on the 30th January!
(3)
The anniversary of the execution of Charles I (1600–1649; King of Great Britain 1625–1649; DNB).
– It appears that Buonaparte
(4)
Napoleon Bonaparte (1769–1821; Emperor of the French 1804–1814, 1815).
was very superstitious with respect to days, – which of all superstitions should seem to be the silliest. This book of Fleury’s
(5)
Pierre Alexandre Édouard, Baron Fleury de Chaboulon (1779–1835), Memoirs of the Private Life, Return, and Reign of Napoleon in 1815, 2 vols (London, 1820), II, p. 295. Fleury had been secretary to Napoleon Bonaparte (1769–1821; Emperor of the French 1804–1814, 1815) in 1815. Southey had a copy of the French edition, Mémoires pour servir à l’Histoire de la Vie Privée: du Retour et du Règne de Napoléon (1819–1820), no. 563 in the sale catalogue of his library; the English translation was published by Murray.
(which like all other books from that squad contains some little new information with a great mass of most impudent falsehood) says that one of his schemes after the second abdication was to get to Spanish America (Mexico if he could) & put himself at the head of the insurgents. But he was dissuaded by the remark that they had heads already, & did not want him.

My daughters Kate & Isabel used when I was going to London to desire that I would bring back with me – “a boy-doll – a male-creature.” They now desire that I will bring back – Willy, – nothing but Willy will satisfy them.

(6)
Rickman’s son, William Charles Rickman (1812–1886).

Remember us all to him & to Franco, & to Mrs Rickman. & I must not forget Miss Anne

(7)
Rickman’s daughters, Anne (b. 1808) and Frances (dates unknown).
– who is no longer to be called little.

Gods bless you
RS.

2 Feby. 1820.
