2379. Robert Southey to Grosvenor Charles Bedford, 18 February 1814

Endorsement: R.S. 18. Feby. 1814
MS: Bodleian Library, MS Eng. Lett. c. 25
Unpublished.

My dear Grosvenor

You have here two books of Roderick,

(1)
MSS drafts of Roderick, the Last of the Goths (1814).
– let the Docstor have them as soon as you can. – And when you have cast your eye over the Ode
(2)
Probably ‘Ode Written During the Negotiations with Bonaparte’, published in the Courier, 3 February 1814.
(if you have not seen it before) put a cover upon it, & dispatch it by the three-penny post to Streatham, – to my Uncle for whom I have thus transcribed it.

I x am looking every day for Rosenhagens frank

(3)
A frank used to send MSS books of Roderick; it came courtesy of Anthony Rosenhagen (1778–1854), financial expert and friend of Landor. He started life as a junior civil servant, but rose swiftly to be private secretary to the Chancellor of the Exchequer 1811–1815.
with the former books, – pray send them with as little delay as can be made to suit your convenience, – for the printer is close upon them, & they will require a few days of reconsideration here.

The 14th book

(4)
Of Roderick, the Last of the Goths.
is finished but not transcribed

RS.

Feby. 18. 1814.
