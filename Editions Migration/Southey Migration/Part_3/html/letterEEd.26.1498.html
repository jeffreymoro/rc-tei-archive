1498. Robert Southey to Grosvenor Charles Bedford, [started before 23 August 1808]

Address: To/ G. C. Bedford Esqr/ Exchequer/ Westminster.
Endorsement: Recd Aug 23 1808.
Stamped: KESWICK/ 298
Postmark: E/ AUG 23/ 1808
MS: Bodleian Library, Eng. Lett. c. 24
Previously published: John Wood Warter (ed.), Selections from the Letters of Robert Southey, 4 vols (London, 1856), II, pp. 82–83.
Dating note: from endorsement

Dear Grosvenor

Recepi, & am, in haste, – for a shoal of Lakers are to drink tea here & minutely expected, – after which I take them on the lake in my boat. I am glad Nicol

(1)
William Nicol (d. c. 1855), publisher, and friend of Grosvenor Bedford’s. He was the son of George Nicol (1740?-1828), in whose publishing firm he became a partner in 1800.
remembers my prophetic feelings about Spain, – of which I am not a little proud. Often & often when expressing that opinion have I seen such a look of incredulity on those present, as if they thought I were saying so merely for the sake of astonishing them, & of seeming to think differently from every body else: – a trick which Gods knows never belongs to me.

I am very-very-most-very-or damned busy, as a man would say who swears, – & be it known that I have no objection to the occasional use of such expletives of emphasis! My old travels

(2)
Letters Written during a Short Residence in Spain and Portugal (1797).
are just come to me to be weeded of some of their faults, – interpolated with later knowledge, – & hurried thro the press as fast as they can go. Sir Richard Phillips that worthy knight
(3)

October 1796], [c. November 1796], [c. December 1796], [c. April 17…
has just published some Travels in Spain & Portugal,
(4)
Travels through Spain, and Part of Portugal, with Commercial, Statistical, and Geographical Details (1808).
in which he has xxx inserted 8 pages of mine from the MMagazine, literatum, – as if they were written by the author of the book himself, – as cool a piece of plagiarism as one shall see of a summers day.

Remember me to your Father & Mother – & to Harry, & tell me how to get Kehama to you – as I mentioned in my last.

(5)
Southey’s poem The Curse of Kehama (1810). For this, see Southey to Grosvenor Charles Bedford, 16 August 1808, Letter 1494.
– My belief is that the downfall of Bonaparte is approaching. The heart of man is against him, & against that there is no power which can stand.

A Dios
RS.
