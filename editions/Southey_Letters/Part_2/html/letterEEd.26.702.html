702. Robert Southey to Charles Biddlecombe, 3 August 1802

Address: To/ Charles Biddlecombe Esqr/ Burton/ Ringwood/
Postmark: [partial] 122/ BRISTOL/ AUG
MS: Berg Collection, New York Public Library
Unpublished.

My dear friend

Your letter inclosing the Bills

(1)
Money raised by the sale of the goods the Southeys had left behind in Burton.
has just arrived – & I write in haste to acknowledge & thank you & Mr Coleman
(2)
Unidentified; presumably an acquaintance of Southey from his residence at Burton.
– for this – & a thousand other acts of kindness

I do not know where the lines

(3)
Biddlecombe’s letter has not survived, so the lines cannot be identified.
you enquire about are to be found. perhaps among Butlers Remains
(4)
Samuel Butler (c. 1613-1680; DNB). Two volumes of his Genuine Remains appeared in 1759.
– that is likely enough – perhaps a scrap designed for some unwritten book of Hudibras.
(5)
Butler’s Hudibras (1663-1678).
more likely still that they may be a proverb – a common sense truism in doggerel rhyme.

Sir Francis Burdetts

(6)
Sir Francis Burdett, 5th Baronet (1770-1844; DNB), had been elected as an MP for Middlesex on 13 July 1802. He was the most outspoken radical in the House of Commons, and his election for a constituency with such a large electorate was taken as a sign of public discontent with the government.
is indeed an important success. it was the test of public opinion in the most important part of the kingdom.

My brother Tom is onshore. he is at Taunton at present – or he would join in remembrances with Edith & myself. we are both well – & what is better – in a few weeks I have the prospect of an increase in family.

(7)
Margaret Edith Southey was born on 31 August 1802.

believe me yours thankfully & truly
Robert Southey.
Tuesday August 3. 1802.
