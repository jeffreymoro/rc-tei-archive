722. Robert Southey to Joseph Cottle [fragment], [c. 30] September 1802

(1)
MS: MS untraced; text is taken from Joseph Cottle, Reminiscences of Samuel Taylor Coleridge and Robert Southey (London, 1847)Previously published: Joseph Cottle, Reminiscences of Samuel Taylor Coleridge and Robert Southey (London, 1847), pp. 227-228.Dating note: written very shortly after Southey’s return from South Wales on c. 28 September 1802; the first surviving letters on his return home date from 30 September, and this letter was written around this time.

Bristol, Sept. 1802.

Dear Cottle,

I was from home, looking out for a habitation in Wales, when your letter arrived. My journey was so far successful, that I am in treaty for a house,

(1)
Maes Gwyn, near Neath; see Southey to Edith Southey [21]-23 September 1802, Letter 720.
eight miles from Neath, in the mountains, a lovely spot, exactly such as will suit my wishes. * * *
