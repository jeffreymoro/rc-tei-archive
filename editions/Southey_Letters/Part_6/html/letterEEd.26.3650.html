3650. Robert Southey to John Abraham Heraud, 13 March 1821

(1)
Address: To/ J. A. Heraud Esqre Junr/ Carey Street/ London Stamped: KESWICK/ 298 Postmark: E/16 MR 16/ 1821 MS: Beinecke Library, Osborn MSS File ‘S’, Folder 14126. ALS; 3p. Unpublished.
Keswick. 13 March. 1821
My dear Sir

Your letter of Feby 3 & the MSS.

(1)
Heraud had sent Southey examples of his unpublished writings.
reached me only this afternoon. In the state of feeling which you describe your own to be I am afraid you will not always have taken into your account of time the probability that this packet should have lain so long at Longmans, waiting for an opportunity of conveyance. I write immediately, that you may <not> look for an answer one day longer, – tho of course it has only been in my power to look at the concluding scenes of the Perjured Son.
(2)
An unpublished piece of work by Heraud.
Tomorrow I shall begin to look at <read> the latest of these compositions. They cannot do more than what the Legend
(3)
Heraud’s The Legend of St Loy, with Other Poems (1820), which had been dedicated to the memory of Henry Kirke White.
has done, – give proof of abundant facility & power. I have no doubt of your power; no doubt of your ultimate success. Only endeavour to calm & regulate your feelings, – to be hopeful, without impatience; – & of all things beware how you contract a habit of watching & analysing analysing your own sensations, – for it is a perilous habit both to body & mind.

I will write to you as soon as I have read the Baron of Kendal.

(4)
Heraud’s ‘The Baron of Kendal’, a historical drama. For Southey’s low opinion of the play see his letter to Heraud, 15 March 1821, Letter 3653. Heraud took this advice and never published this work.
This letter is merely to relieve you from anxiety, & to assure you of the interest which I take in your welfare.

God bless you.Yrs very truly
Robert Southey.

My poem came with your packet. I trust therefore that you have received a copy. – In the last line of the first page, the metre is spoiled by the omission of a letter in the last word, which ought to be Glaramara.

(5)
The correction is to A Vision of Judgement (London, 1821), Canto 1, line 9.
