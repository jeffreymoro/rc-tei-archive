3761. Robert Southey to Henry Herbert Southey, 12 December 1821

Address: To/ Dr Southey/ 15. Queen Anne Street/ Cavendish Square/ London.
Stamped: KESWICK/ 298
Postmark: E/ 15 DE 15/ 1821
MS: Keswick Museum and Art Gallery, KESMG 1996.5.120. ALS; 2p.
Unpublished.

My dear Harry

As you think venison worth carriage, I have shipt off for you a consignment thereof, just as it reached me from Lowther this morning. That house is become a very convenient one for me; – it supplies me liberally with game during the season, & I have moreover the use of the library I have borrowed Rushworth & Nalson

(1)
John Rushworth (1612–1690; DNB), Historical Collections of Private Passages of State (1659–1721); John Nalson (1637–1686; DNB), An Impartial Collection of the Great Affairs of State, from the Beginning of the Scotch Rebellion in the Year MDCXXXIX to the Murther of King Charles I (1682–1683), no. 1924 in the sale catalogue of Southey’s library. Southey did not write his planned life of Oliver Cromwell (1599–1658; Lord Protector 1653–1658; DNB).
from thence, to make preparations for an elaborate life of Cromwell.

This is an O be joyful

(2)
The first line of the Jubilate Deo, part of the Anglican service of Morning Prayer.
evening, – that is, – I shall finish a paper for the Q.R.
(3)
Southey reviewed Sara Coleridge’s An Account of the Abipones, an Equestrian People of Paraguay (1822) in Quarterly Review, 26 (January 1822), 277–323. The book was a translation of Martin Dobrizhoffer (1717–1791), Historia de Abiponibus Equestri, Bellicosaque Paraquariae Natione (1784).
An ode for poor Shields is my next dogged undertaking,
(4)
Southey’s New Year’s Ode for 1822, as Poet Laureate: ‘Ireland’, published in Sir Thomas More: or, Colloquies on the Progress and Prospects of Society, 2 vols (London, 1829), I, pp. [295]–302.
– & then the Book of the Church
(5)
Southey’s The Book of the Church (1824).
goes to the Press.

Love to all. God bless you.
RS.

Keswick 12 Dec. 1821.
