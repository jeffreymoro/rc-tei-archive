3310. Robert Southey to John Rickman, 5 June [1819]

(1)
MS: Huntington Library, RS 369. ALS; 2p. Unpublished.
My dear R.

Your note has placed me in a dilemma, the possibility of which I had put out of sight.

(1)
Southey had been intending to visit London, but Rickman proposed that they should visit Scotland together.
My stay in the south cannot be for a shorter time than two full months, so wide must my excursions be, & so manifold my occupations. I have then to determine between putting off this journey till the fall of the leaf, – & giving up my long intended hazard of catching the scratchums in your company.
(2)
An effect of the midges prevalent in the Scottish Highlands in summer.
And this requires some consideration. – If it be true that when men begin to consider how they shall act, they always end in conformity to their inclination, I shall be of your party. But I will decide & tell you in a few days.

You lent me Fletcher of Salou Saltouns speculations upon slavery two & twenty years ago;

(3)
Andrew Fletcher of Saltoun (1655–1716; DNB), Two Discourses Concerning the Affairs of Scotland (1698), which argued for the enslavement of vagrants.
– arguments must have something in them which are remembered after so long a lapse of time: There is a note about population in some of the ensuing sheets, wherein if I have committed any error you will correct me.
(4)
History of Brazil, 3 vols (London, 1810–1819), III, pp. 837–838.
– I am very very near the end, – within a fortnight; – & have therefore indulged myself with writing the peroration.

Remember us to Mrs R.

God bless you
RS

Sunday 5 June.
