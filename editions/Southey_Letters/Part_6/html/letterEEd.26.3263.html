3263. Robert Southey to George Ticknor, 11 March 1819

(1)
Address: To/ George Ticknor Esqre/ with Walter Scott Esqre/ Abbotsford/ Melrose –/ Scotland Stamped: KESWICK/ 298 Endorsement: Southey MS: Rauner Special Collections Library, Dartmouth College Library, Ticknor 819211.1. (c). ALS; 2p. Unpublished.
Keswick.March 11. 1819.
My dear Sir

I am at home, & shall be truly glad to see you, for as long a time as you can make it convenient to stay. It is not in my power to offer you a bed, as I should otherwise have done, – because owing to the recent birth

(1)
Charles Cuthbert Southey was born on 24 February 1819.
of a child I am myself quartered in the strangers room, – but the inn is within five minutes walk, & it will be give me great pleasure to offer you all other offices of hospitality.

I had forgotten your name till I recovered it from Mr Everett in the spring of last year.

(2)
Southey had met Ticknor in Paris on 18 May 1817, at the house of Helen Maria Williams.
Nothing else had slipt my remembrance.

yrs faithfully
Robert Southey.
