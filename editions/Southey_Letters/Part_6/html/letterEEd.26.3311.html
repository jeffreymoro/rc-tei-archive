3311. Robert Southey to Grosvenor Charles Bedford, 10 June 1819

(1)
Address: To/ G. C. Bedford Esqre/ Exchequer Endorsements: 10 June 1819; 10 June 1819 MS: Bodleian Library, MS Eng. Lett. d. 47. ALS; 3p. Unpublished.
My Dear G.

To your wish – I must put off my southwards, – but for more than a month. – I had to chuse between this prorogation, & giving up an engagement which for three years had been put off year by year, from different causes (last year because of the Queens illness)

(1)
Queen Charlotte (1744–1818; DNB) had died on 17 November 1818.
to meet Rickman in Scotland, & accompanying him to the Highlands Canal.
(2)
Rickman was Secretary to the Commission responsible for constructing the Caledonian Canal 1803–1822.
His journey cannot be delayed another year – & so I alter my arrangements accordingly. This is in some respects unlucky, – but it suits well with Wesley
(3)
Southey’s The Life of Wesley; and the Rise and Progress of Methodism (1820).
, & with my finances, which will be recruited by this respite.

Mrs Hemans is certainly no common writer. Her last volume is on the lathe.

(4)
Felicia Hemans (1793–1835; DNB), Tales and Historic Scenes (1819). She had just separated from her husband, and so was in need of a successful publication. The book was reviewed, with others, by John Taylor Coleridge, in Quarterly Review, 24 (October 1820), 130–139.
By all means review it if you can, – for it will be of great service to her, & I believe she is in circumstances which will render such service very acceptable to her. I had a letter from her some years ago.

Gifford has cut out the only good thing in my paper – about Christian in the P Progress & Mr Legality & Mr By-ends.

(5)
Southey’s ‘Inquiry into the Copyright Act’, Quarterly Review, 21 (January 1819), 196–213, published 4 June 1819. These references to John Bunyan (1628–1688; DNB), Pilgrim’s Progress (1678), in which Mr By-ends is a false pilgrim and Mr Legality an unhelpful guide, were removed.
I have a letter to day from his friend Davison,
(6)
John Davison (1777–1834; DNB), clergyman and writer for the Quarterly Review.
saying he shall call upon me soon.

So I shall come to town in November

God bless you
RS.

June 10. 1819
