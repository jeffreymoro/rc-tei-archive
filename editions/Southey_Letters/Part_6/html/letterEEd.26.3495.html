3495. Robert Southey to [Unknown Correspondent], [19 June 1820]

MS: Beinecke Library, Osborn MSS File ‘S’, Folder 14202. ALS; 1p.
Unpublished.
Dating note: Correspondence detailing Southey’s itinerary at this time indicates that this letter was probably written on Monday 19 June 1820.

Dear Sir

It would have given me sincere pleasure to accept your invitation for Thursday, were I not engaged to go to Tunbridge

(1)
Southey’s sister-in-law Louisa Southey, and her mother, Mary Gonne, were staying in Tunbridge at this time.
on that day. On Wednesday I will avail myself of your kindness & call in the morning, as soon as I can reach town from Battersea Rise
(2)
Southey’s correspondent Sir Robert Inglis Harry lived in Battersea Rise, in the southern part of London.

I remain dear Sir Yr obliged & obedient
Robert Southey

Monday afternoon.
