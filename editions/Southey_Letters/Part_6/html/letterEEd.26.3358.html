3358. Robert Southey to Herbert Hill, 6 October 1819

Address: [in another hand] Keswick Sixth Octr 1819./ Revd. Herbert Hill/ Streatham/ near/ London/ J Rickman
Stamped: KESWICK/ 298
Postmarks: [partial] 10 o’Clock/ OC. 9/ F Nn/; FREE/ 9 OC 9/ 1819
MS: Keswick Museum and Art Gallery, WC 187. ALS; 3p.
Unpublished.

Keswick. 6 Oct. 1819.

I was not a little surprized at learning on my return that the publication of my xxx xxxx unlucky volume

(1)
The third and final volume of Southey’s History of Brazil (1810–1819).
was delayed, owing to the loss of a proof sheet, & the want of brains in all persons concerned with it, – for if they had sent the sheet to you, the delay might have been prevented. – Whatever the mss from Para
(2)
When the parcel containing this work arrived, Southey found it to be nothing more than another copy of Manoel Aires de Casal (1754–1821), Corografia Brazilica, ou Relação Historico-Geografica do Reino do Brazil (1817), no. 3252 in the sale catalogue of Southey’s library.
may contain, it would not be advisable to introduce it now, – a good deal may be expected from it – which I shall take care to have ready for insertion in its proper place, whether a time for so doing may occur during my life, or not. Koster has sent me over a paper upon concerning the same part of the country, written by a man
(3)
Probably a paper by Thomaz Antonio Maciel Monteiro (1780–1847), Brazilian lawyer, later a member of the Supreme Court and 1st Baron Itamarca.
who is a native of Para & held the office of Procurador there, – but is now Juiz di Fora at Paraiba. The manner is sadly Frenchified, – but there are some facts which I wish I had obtained sooner.

Lord Lonsdale sent to me to meet Prince Leopold at Lowther.

(4)
Prince Leopold of Saxe-Coburg (1790–1865; King of the Belgians 1831–1865; DNB), widower of Princess Charlotte, had visited Lord Lonsdale at Lowther Castle 25–28 September 1819; he met ‘a numerous party of the gentry in the neighbourhood’ and received the Freedom of the Borough of Appleby, Morning Chronicle, 2 October 1819.
I am not sorry that I did not reach home till a week after the expiration of his visit.

The Rickmans

(5)
John and Susannah Rickman and their children.
left me this morning on their way to town, where they will arrive in about a week. We have had a prosperous journey, without let or accident, & with weather uninterruptedly fine.
(6)
Southey’s tour of Scotland lasted from 17 August until 1 October 1819. For his record of events, see Journal of a Tour in Scotland in 1819, ed. Charles Harold Herford (1929).
My head is nearly healed, – I shall be glad when it is quite so, that I may be able to clean it thoroughly. I found all at home in tolerable health, – Cuthbert quite strong & thriving.

I must now fall to work, – upon Wesley,

(7)
Southey’s The Life of Wesley; and the Rise and Progress of Methodism (1820).
& the Peninsular War.
(8)
Southey’s History of the Peninsular War (1823–1832).
The former will not require much time, – about a third of the second volume is printed.

Love to my Aunt –

God bless you
RS.
