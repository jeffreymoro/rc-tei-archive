2887. Robert Southey to [Samuel Rogers], 23 December 1816

(1)
MS: University College, London, Special Collections, Sharpe Papers 208, Album of Letters and Autographs Collected by Sarah Rogers, f. 124 Unpublished.
Keswick.23d Dec. 1816
My dear Sir

I thank you most sincerely for your very kind reply to my application in behalf of Herbert Knowles,

(1)
Southey had written to Rogers on 13 December 1816 (Letter 2879), asking him to contribute a sum to allow the young poet Herbert Knowles to attend St John’s College, Cambridge. Knowles died before he could take advantage of Rogers’s generosity.
– I have written to inform him of his good fortune, There are no prizes in the after lottery of life which will give him equal delight; – it will bring out his hopes into full blossom, – & God bless the fruitage!

Present my warm thanks to Lord Spencer,

(2)
George John Spencer, 2nd Earl Spencer (1758–1834; DNB), with whom Samuel Rogers was staying when Southey’s request arrived, also agreed to contribute to Knowles’s expenses at Cambridge.
– for indeed I feel this as a personal obligation. – The remittances had better be made toward Midsummer, – when the half-yearly bills become due, – & they may be addressed for the ensuing year to the youth himself, at the Revd James Tates, Richmond Yorkshire.

Believe me my dear Siryrs with the greatest esteem & respect
Robert Southey.
