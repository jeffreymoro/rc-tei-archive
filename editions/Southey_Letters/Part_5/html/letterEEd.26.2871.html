2871. Robert Southey to John Rickman [fragment], [November 1816]

(1)
Address: [partial] To/ John Rick/ St Step/ N Endorsement: Novr. 1816. MS: Huntington Library, RS 298. AL; 2p. Unpublished. Dating note: Dating from endorsement.
My dear R.

I find in Bp. Burnet that in 1704 the poor rates in most places amounted to a full half of the public taxes.

(1)
Gilbert Burnet (1643–1715; DNB), Bishop of Salisbury 1689–1715, Bishop Burnet’s History of His Own Time, 4 vols (London, 1809), IV, p. 54.
– Surely in proportion to the wealth & taxation of the country things were w this was worse than it is now.

And now I gird up my loins.

(2)
Southey intended to write a book on the ‘State of the Nation’.
