3132. Robert Southey to Grosvenor Charles Bedford,9 May 1818

Endorsements: 9 May 1818; 9 May 1818/ from R.S.
MS: Bodleian Library, MS Eng. Lett. d. 47. ALS; 3p.
Unpublished.

My dear G.

When you see Nash compel him to receive payment for certain frames, & some drawing paper, & watch-mending – which altogether may amount to between 15 & 20 £, – & N.B. that the frame for Westalls view from Saddle-back be included in the account.

(1)
Westall’s ‘Keswick Lake, from Saddleback’. An aquatint engraving from this painting was included in Views of the Lake and of the Vale of Keswick (1820).

I should be truly glad to hear that these long looked for books

(2)
A consignment of books that Southey had bought in Brussels in 1817.
were fairly lodged in the Row. Thither they must go, & I will send up directions concerning them. the Acta
(3)
Acta Sanctorum (1643–1794), a 53–volume compendium of hagiographies; it was no. 207 in the sale catalogue of Southey’s library.
need lettering, & perhaps a little conservative assistance aid from the bookbinder; & there are a few books which I purchased for John May. But when once they are in the Row, I will desire Nash to look to all this, because he will know the few which belong to Senhouse, & I shall {have no} need to give you no farther trouble than that of discharging the freight & other expences, which Rothschild
(4)
Southey had recruited the banker Nathan Mayer Rothschild (1777–1836; DNB) to help with the transit of his books.
may have paid. I am so much obliged to this personage, that if he were not so great a personage I should feel “unpleasant as a Gentleman” till I could find {obtain} an opportunity of expressing my thanks.

Tell Osiris that the carpet is arrived, & that we like it much. His & your old friend the ipsissimus John Cockbaine

(5)
‘The man himself’: John Cockbain (1786–1873), Keswick draper and tailor.
was here on Thursday, with three aides-des camp to make it.

What is become of the Q.R?

(6)
Quarterly Review, 18 (January 1818) was not published until 9 June 1818.

I have two proofs by this post, – one of Brazil, one of Wesley,

(7)
The third and final volume of Southey’s History of Brazil (1810–1819) and The Life of Wesley; and the Rise and Progress of Methodism (1820).
– judge whether there is work enough on my hands. Tomorrow or next day I shall send you a second portion of MS. of the latter. It reads well in the proofs.

We are not so forward in the season as you are. The cold weather continued with us till Monday 27th. it left you on the 25th. Every thing is now opening into life & beauty, & there is a great promise of – gooseberries, – the only crop in which I have any direct interest.

Remember my bill to Hyde

(8)
Hyde (d. 1820) was Southey’s London tailor.
when you go that way.

God bless you
RS.

9th May. Keswick.
