2978. Robert Southey to John Rickman, [19 April 1817]

(1)
Endorsement: RS./ [illegible, possibly 21 Apr?] 1817 MS: Huntington Library, RS 319. ALS; 2p. Unpublished. Dating note: Dating from content.
My dear R.

Since I reached Warcop I have had reason to fear that my further progress would be st prevented, – for I have been seized with an inflammation in the bowels – I hope it is now gone off.

I am unluckily engaged on Saturday the 26, – & precisely because it is not a Parliament day: – Harry Inglis having written some weeks ago to fix that day for meeting Lord Sidmouth

(1)
Henry Addington, 1st Viscount Sidmouth (1757–1844; DNB), Prime Minister 1801–1804, Home Secretary 1812–1822.
& Wilberforce at his house. Shall I come to you on Sunday? – for on the Saturday following I expect to be over the seas & far away.

Poor Sharon is obliged to give up all evening company from an asthmatic complaint, which excitement after dinner never fails to bring on.

In haste to save the post.

God bless you
RS.

Saturday morning
