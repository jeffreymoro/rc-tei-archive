3025. Robert Southey to [John Baker Holroyd, 1st Earl of Sheffield], 30 September 1817

MS: Beinecke Library, Osborn MSS File ‘S’, Folder 14158. ALS; 1p.
Unpublished.

Keswick. 30th Sept. 1817
My Lord

I return the papers which I had the pleasure of receiving from your Lordship on Sunday evening: – they are under four covers.

(1)
To aid Southey’s researches towards his History of the Peninsular War (1823–1832), Sheffield had supplied him with papers from the British campaign in Iberia, mainly from his son-in-law, General Sir William Henry Clinton (1769–1846; DNB), commander of the 1st Division in Spain 1812–1813 and commander-in-chief of British forces in eastern Spain 1813–1814.
The details are very interesting. More enterprize & more ability were displayed by the Catalans than by any other part of the Spanish nation, – & this was to be expected from the character which they have always maintained.

I have the honour to be

My Lord with great respect your Lordships obliged & faithful servant
Robert Southey.
