2913. Robert Southey to John Rickman, [c. 2 February 1817]

(1)
Endorsement: RS/ Febry 2 1817 MS: Huntington Library, RS 310. ALS; 1p. Unpublished. Dating note: Dating from endorsement
My dear R.

I like your hint about the New Times, & write therefore to Bedford for the MS. in which alone the mutilated facts exist. There is stout preachment there about the wisdom of expenditure, – at which faint hearts were frightened.

(1)
Southey’s article ‘Parliamentary Reform’, Quarterly Review, 16 (October 1816), 225–278 argued strongly for the value of increased government expenditure to the economy (277–278), but Southey indicates more of his argument was cut out. Rickman had suggested Southey should make the same point at greater length and without editorial censorship in the new pro-government newspaper, The New Times (1817–1828).
But they suffered the text to stand that it was the power of the democracy which had increased &c –.
(2)
‘At present it is the influence of the democracy which has increased, is increasing, and ought to be diminished’, ‘Parliamentary Reform’, Quarterly Review, 16 (October 1816), 252.

This note to Wakefield

(3)
Possibly Edward Wakefield (1774–1854; DNB), philanthropist and statistician.
is in reply to an offer made thro him by Lord Sheffield of papers respecting the operations in the South of Spain.
(4)
For Southey’s reply see his letter to John Baker Holroyd, 1st Earl of Sheffield, 8 May 1817, Letter 2990.

God bless you
RS.
