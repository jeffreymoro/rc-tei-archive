3196. Robert Southey to John Rickman,18 September 1818

Address: To/ John Rickman Esqre/ St Stephens Court/ New Palace Yard/ Westminster
Stamped: KESWICK/ 298
Postmark: FREE/ 21 SE 21/ 1818
MS: Huntington Library, RS 353. ALS; 2p.
Unpublished.

My dear R.

I have been turning over the leaves of the Acta Sanctorum,

(1)
The massive compendium of hagiographies entitled Acta Sanctorum (1643–1794) which Southey had bought in Brussels in 1817, but which had just arrived; no. 207 in the sale catalogue of Southey’s library.
just to look at the prints & glance at the contents, {& having done this,} I am about, in horis otiosioribus,
(2)
‘In leisure hours’.
to go thro all that they contain concerning this island, Ireland & Britanny, as connected therewith. This is merely for the sake of the gleanings which I can hardly fail to gather in such a field. – But the main use of these tremendous volumes will be for a work projected eighteen years ago upon the M[MS missing] Orders; – a regular history of them (which I then thought of) will be beyond my powers, – but under the title of Sketches of Monastic History, I may take the cream of the subject.
(3)
Southey’s ‘History of the Monastic Orders’ was never completed.
You know I have a good custom of brooding over a work long before I undertake it & thus imperceptibly the design matures & shapes itself.

My third vol. of Brazil is now half printed, – & of the remaining half more than half the work is done.

(4)
The History of Brazil (1810–1819).
– I see by my years account that half the second volume are sold, & of the first volume 102 copies are left
(5)
750 copies of the first volume of the History of Brazil were printed in 1810; and 750 copies of the second volume in 1817.
– When the concluding volume is published the whole of the first will soon be carried off,
(6)
The third volume of the History of Brazil was printed in 1819; but the first volume did not sell out until mid–1820.
– but I fear there will be a good many first volumes which will never be joined to their intended companions. As soon as this Opus is out of the Press, the peninsular war goes in,
(7)
The History of the Peninsular War (1823–1832).
– & as soon as the Life of Welsey
(8)
The Life of Wesley; and the Rise and Progress of Methodism (1820).
is compleated, I shall put together the long gathered & still accumulating materials of Espriella: wherein {thro that vehicle} much may be said.
(9)
Southey never published a second instalment of the travel narrative of his fictional visitor to Britain, Letters from England: By Don Manuel Alvarez Espriella (1807).

Remember me to Mrs. R.

God bless you
RS.

18 Sept. 1818
