2774. Robert Southey to Thomas Southey, 27 April [1816]

(1)
Address: To/ Capt Southey RN/ Warcop Hall/ near Appleby Stamped: KESWICK/ 298 MS: British Library, Add MS 47890. ALS; 2p. Unpublished. Dating note: Dating from content, this was written shortly after the death of Herbert Southey; 27 April fell on Saturday in 1816.
Saturday 27 April.
My dear Tom

I received the books to day. – There can be no possible objection to my sending you any or all of the books, which come to me from Murray: – & indeed you are near enough now to receive also a regular supply of such as are my own. The only difficulty about Murrays is to know when I have done with them.

(1)
Southey refers to books sent to him to aid him in writing his reviews in the Quarterly Review.
xxxx

You will be glad to hear that I am better in bodily health, & that I employ myself assiduously, & with good effect. I was very much reduced in strength, but am regaining it, by the help of tonics & regular exercise. I have the best consolation – & know how to make the best use of it, – & I have many blessings left, – great & abundant blessings. I am therefore thankful & resigned. May you never experience a like affliction,

(2)
The death of his son Herbert on 17 April 1816.
– but if it should be so allotted, may you derive the xxxx {same} consolation from the same source. – I cannot speak of Ediths conduct in higher terms than it deserves.

Love to Sarah.

God bless you
RS.
