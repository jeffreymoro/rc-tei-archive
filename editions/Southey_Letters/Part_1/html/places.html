Places

This page gives further information about the key places that Southey lived in, visited or was connected to during the period 1791-1797.


  • Balliol College, Oxford: Southey matriculated at Balliol in November 1792 and went into residence in January 1793. He finally left without taking a degree in July 1794.

  • Brixton, a village just outside London: the country home of the Bedford family, Southey spent several weeks there in August–October 1793, during which time he wrote the first draft of Joan of Arc.

  • Burton: Southey rented a cottage at Burton, a village in Hampshire, from June-September 1797.

  • College Green, Bristol: the home of Southey’s aunt, Elizabeth Tyler. He spent several years of his childhood living there and often returned in 1792-4. In October 1794, Miss Tyler threw Southey out of the house when she learned of his relationship with Edith Fricker and involvement in Pantisocracy.

  • 25 College St, Bristol: a house rented by Southey, Coleridge and George Burnett in January–August 1795.

  • 9 Duke St, Bath: the residence of Southey’s parents; it was given up in early 1793, a few months after the death of Southey’s father.

  • Maize Hill, Greenwich near London: home of Charles Collins and his family.

  • Mountsfield Lodge, Rye: the home of Thomas Phillipps Lamb and his family. Southey visited the Lambs there in 1791 and 1792.

  • No. 20 Prospect Place, Newington Butts: Southey’s residence in London during February–May 1797. His landlords were a Mr and Mrs Peacock.

  • Sapey, Worcestershire: the home of the Seward family. Southey visited in March-April 1793.

  • Theobalds, Waltham Cross, Hertfordshire: the home of Southey’s friend Mrs Louisa Dolignon and her sisters the Misses Delamere. Southey spent much time there when he was at Westminster School and stayed at Theobalds after his expulsion from Westminster in April 1792.

  • 8 Westgate Buildings, Bath: the boarding house run by Margaret Southey from 1793.

  • Wynnstay, near Ruabon, Denbighshire: the home of the Wynn family.
