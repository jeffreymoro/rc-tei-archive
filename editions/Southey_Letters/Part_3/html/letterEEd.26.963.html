963. Robert Southey to Grosvenor Charles Bedford, 13 July 1804

Address: To/ G.C. Bedford Esqr/ Exchequer/ Westminster/ Single
Stamped: KESWICK/ 298
Postmark: E/ JUL16/1804
Endorsement: 13 July 1804
MS: Bodleian Library, MS Eng. Lett. c. 23
Unpublished.

Grosvenor I am very uneasy at receiving no tidings of or from you – or Horace. write – if only to tell me the reason of your silence.

Somewhat oddly it has happened that I have been told here by one who professed to know accurately, & perhaps had the means of knowing, that your father is worth one & twenty thousand pounds. It was Sharp who said this, & as if on Isaac Reids

(1)
Isaac Reed (1742–1807; DNB), a conveyancer, editor and literary historian. He was the author of Biographia Dramatica, or, a Companion to the Playhouse (1782) and editor of Shakespeare’s plays. A friend of Sharp, both men were members of the Unincreasable Club in Holborn.
authority.

pray write!
RS.

July 13. 1804. Keswick

Notes
1.. Isaac Reed (1742–1807; DNB), a conveyancer, editor and literary historian. He was the author of Biographia Dramatica, or, a Companion to the Playhouse (1782) and editor of Shakespeare’s plays. A friend of Sharp, both men were members of the Unincreasable Club in Holborn.[back]
