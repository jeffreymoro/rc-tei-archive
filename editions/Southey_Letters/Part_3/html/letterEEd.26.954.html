954. Robert Southey to John Rickman, [c. 16 June 1804]

(1)
Address: To/ John Rickman EsqrEndorsement: RS./ June circa 16:/ 1804MS: Huntington Library, RS 59Unpublished.
Dear Rickman

I do not like making so free with you as to make free with the Emperor of the Franks

(1)
‘Southey’s nickname for the Speaker of the House of Commons, Charles Abbott, who had the power to frank mail. Rickman, his secretary, used this facility on Southey’s behalf.
for my own convenience. but the inclosed relates to the drawings for Madoc,
(2)
On the drawings Southey commissioned his friends to provide for engraving as illustrations to Madoc (1805), see letters 897, 904, 906 and 1010 in this edition.
& it would neither be proper to tax Duppa with a double letter on my business nor yet decorous to say post paid on the direction.

There was a line in Madoc about a cat-a-mountain – which so many objected to that I have altered it to some mountain marten in honour <remembrance> of your favourite.

(3)
Madoc (1805), Part 1, Book 12, lines 121–122: ‘the bonnet last, / Formed of some forest martin’s costly spoils’.

We go on well – I, Edith & the Edithling. only when the child starts at a light sound, as she frequently does – it gives me an ominous apprehension lest the brain be prematurely susceptible.

RS.
