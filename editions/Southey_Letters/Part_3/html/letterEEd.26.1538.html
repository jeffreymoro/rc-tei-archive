1538. Robert Southey to Grosvenor Charles Bedford, 20 November 1808

Address: To/ G. C. Bedford Esqr./ Exchequer
Endorsement: 20 Novr 1808
MS: Bodleian Library, Eng. Lett. c. 24
Unpublished.

You do not seem yet to have suspected that Kehama

(1)
The title character of Southey’s poemThe Curse of Kehama (1810).
was the Butler.
(2)
A character in the comic and whimsical stories and jests, that Southey and Bedford had composed as schoolboys at Westminster.
Bear in mind with you that no lame or inharmonious versification will be suffered to stand when the poem comes to be corrected, & that a great deal of ornament will be woven in. I am getting on in a good vein of story & in good humour with it.

RS.

Nov. 20. 1808
