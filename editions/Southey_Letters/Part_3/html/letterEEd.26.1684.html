1684. Robert Southey to John Rickman, 22 September [1809]

(1)
Address: To/ John Rickman Esqr.Endorsement: From/ RS./ 22 Septr. 1809 MS: Huntington Library, RS 145Unpublished.
My dear Rickman

I was about to fold up these the concluding Chapters under cover to Perceval,

(1)
The first volume of Southey’s History of Brazil, published in 1810.
– when it occurred to me that he might no longer be in Spain by this time. Where you are, or where the Emperor
(2)
A nickname for the Speaker of the House of Commons, Charles Abbott, who had the power to frank mail. Rickman, his secretary, used this facility on Southey’s behalf.
is I know not: but this will find him, & if when it finds you, you have not the Emperor at hand to frank it on, – send it in to Bedford, – & he will devise some means of getting a name potential on the cover.

RevdH. Hill

Staunton-upon Wye

Hereford

I am putting together the additional notes & the Bibliographical Appendix.

Has Aaron seen the maps to Azaras Voyage?

(3)
Felix Manuel de Azara (1742–1821), Voyages dans l’Amerique Meridionale depuis 1781, jusqu’ en 1801 (1809), no. 90 in the sale catalogue of Southey’s library.

I have lost all hope of seeing you this year – & am now not sorry for the disappointment (were it not for the cause) – for our weather having been uninterruptedly bad for the last six weeks.

Duppa will be here for three days next week.

I begin to be impatient for the black letter title page. You I hope will think my book as good as the subject admits of. The next volume will be more interesting, & (I hope) less laborious than this has proved.

(4)
The second volume of Southey’s History of Brazil was published in 1817.

farewell
RS

Friday Sept. 22.
