4292. Robert Southey to Susannah Rickman, 12 December 1824

(1)
Address: To/ Mrs Rickman Endorsement: R Southey to SR/ 24 MS: The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Wilson Library, James Saxon Childers Papers. ALS; 3p. Unpublished.
Keswick.12 Dec. 1824
My dear Madam

I must not send off the conclusion of a five weeks journal,

(1)
A fair copy of the journal Southey kept on his trip to the Low Countries in 1815. It was later published as Journal of a Tour in the Netherlands in the Autumn of 1815 (1902).
without once more thanking you for all your kindness to Bertha. She will bring home with her much improvement of every kind, as well as a large stock of delightful recollections. Her visit has extended to an unreasonable length of time, – but I hope that some convoy will be found for her & her sister in the course of February. We are beginning to be a little impatient of their long absence. The house does not seem like itself in winter evenings without them.

My next Journal

(2)
This journal described Southey’s trip to France, Italy, Switzerland, Germany and the Low Countries in May–August 1817.
enters upon a wider circuit, & thro a greater variety of scenes. I shall begin to transcribe it without delay, glad to have a motive for so doing in the hope that it may afford you some entertainment.

With the kindest remembrances from Mrs S. & her sisters.

(3)
Sara Coleridge and Mary Lovell.

I remain Dear Madam Yrs very faithfully
Robert Southey.
