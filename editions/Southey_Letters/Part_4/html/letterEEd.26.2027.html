2027. Robert Southey to Herbert Hill, 1 February 1812

(1)
Address: To / The Reverend Herbert Hill / StreathamPostmarks: [partial] o’Clock/ 4 FE/ 1812 NnMS: Keswick Museum and Art GalleryUnpublished.

My advertisement for Books

(1)
History of Brazil, 3 vols (London, 1810–1819), I, p. vi.
has done its work effectually at last. Mr James Gooden,
(2)
Southey later publicly thanked Gooden for ‘the Life of F. Joam d’Almeida, among other books, and a manuscript Apology for the Jesuits in Paraguay and Maranham, of great importance’, see Southey’s History of Brazil, 3 vols (London, 1810–1819), II, p. [v].
who has been living at Lisbon in the house with Sealey,
(3)
Richard Sealy (d. 1821), Lisbon merchant and father-in-law of Henry Herbert Southey.
collected books & papers in Brazil & has offered me the use of them. Among the books there are three which will be of service, – Collecçaõ dos Breves Pontificios &c sobre as liberdades das pessoas, bens &c dos Indios do Brazil.
(4)
Colleccao dos Breves Pontificios, e Leys Regias, que Formo Expedidos, e Publicadas desde o anno de 1741, sobre as liberdades das Pessoas, Bens, e Commercio dos Indios do Brasil (n.d.), no. 3359 in the sale catalogue of Southey’s library.
Chronica dos Frades Minores da Provincia do Brazil por Fr. Ant: de S. Maria Jaboataõ,
(5)
Antonio de Santa Maria Jaboatao (1695–1763/1765), Novo Orbe Serafico Brazilico ou Chronica dos Frades Minores da Provincia do Brazil (1761).
– one of the books for which you wrote to Lisbon in vain, – & the long sought for life of P. Joam de Almeida,
(6)
Simon de Vasconcellos (1599–1670), Vida do Joam d’Almeida (1658). This was a life of the English-born Jesuit missionary, John Almeida (1571–1653).
which of all books in the world is the one I wanted the most. Of his MSS I have only asked at present for a defence of the Jesuits,
(7)
Unidentified. This is one of the works that Southey thanked Gooden for supplying in History of Brazil, 3 vols (London, 1810–1819), II, p. [v].
written about the time of Pombal’s
(8)
Sebastiao Jose de Carvalho e Melo, Marquess of Pombal (1699–1782), Prime Minister of Portugal 1750–1777.
disgrace, & confined (this volume) to the provinces of Paraguay & Maranhaõ. But he has many others which will be useful. Participaçaõ Geral do Rio Negro &c – pelo Naturalista Al Ruiz Ferreira.
(9)
This work was probably a manuscript account of the travels of the naturalist Alexandre Rodrigues Ferreira (1756–1815) in north-central Brazil in 1783–1792.

Diario de Viagem que a expediçaõ destinada a demarcaçao &c fez so Rio Negro até Villa Bella &c.

(10)
Possibly Francisco Xavier Ribeiro de Sampaio (1741–1812?), Diario da Viagem que em visita e correicao das provoacoes da capitana de S. Jose do Rio Negro fez o ouvidor e intendente geral da mesma no ano de 1774 e 1775, no. 3859 in the sale catalogue of Southey’s library. This was a manuscript of a work that was not published until 1825.
Besides others which he tells Robinson he could lend with a verbal explanation. I have not asked him for any of these yet, because the others will be as much as I can get through for some time, & are what will be wanted first. Perhaps when you go to town you will call upon him & get sight of them. He lives in Swithins Lane, but has not given me the number, however as he is a Merchant, there can be no difficulty in discovering him. You might get the papers into your hands, & some of them may be worth transcribing – Robinson speaks very highly of Goodens manners & conversation, – he was xxx educated in Germany for a mercantile life & there acquired a love of letters.

I have no time for more, – except to say that I shall look anxiously for news of my unborn cousin. Our love to my Aunt, – I hope the Man & his brother are as well as their Welsh Nephew & nieces.

RS.

Feby 1. 1812. Keswick
