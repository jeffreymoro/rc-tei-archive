2535. Robert Southey to John Rickman, 31 December [1814]

(1)
Address: To/ John Rickman Esqre/ St Stephens Court/ New Palace Yard/ WestminsterEndorsement: RS./ 31 Decr. 1814Stamped: KESWICK/ 298Postmarks: FREE/ 3 JA 3/ 1815 MS: Huntington Library, RS 241Unpublished.
My dear R.

I have written some worthless rhymes for performance at court – the sin be upon those who made me write them.

(1)
Southey’s second attempt at a New Year’s ode for 1815, ‘The palm of peace is won’, which only exists in one draft version, dated ‘29 Dec. 1814’ in his notebook, now Huntington MS 2733, ff. 16v-17r.
– I am glad of the peace with America,
(2)
The Treaty of Ghent, signed 24 December 1814, ended the War of 1812 between Britain and America.
– as terminating a war in which we did not go the way to get the only things which could be got by it; honour & vengeance. – They say the Income Tax will be dropt;
(3)
The war-time income tax was finally abolished in 1816.
– it is a wicked tax – but what can be substituted for it? Better however any thing than this.

Something is likely to come out of the gasses at last, some experiments related in the Monthly Magazine for this month seem to show that they may furnish the best & easiest means of preserving meat.

(4)
Monthly Magazine, 38 (December 1814), 460.

Many & happy new years to you –
RS.

31 Decr.
