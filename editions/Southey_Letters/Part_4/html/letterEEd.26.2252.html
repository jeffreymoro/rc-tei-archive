2252. Robert Southey to John Rickman, [April 1813]

Endorsement: Southey to Mr Rickman
MS: University of Kentucky Library; a copy in another hand (endorsed ‘Given to Mr Long’) is Huntington Library, RS 199
Unpublished.
Dating note: Dated from content.

I hope you have received Nelson.

(1)
The Life of Nelson (1813).
I directed one for the Capitaneus also.

What a situation was that in the vault at Windsor. – The eye of Charles I open in the <his> coffin after more than a century & half, that it might look upon the Prince Regent & then close for ever!

(2)
On 1 April 1813, the Prince Regent had attended the opening of the coffin of Charles I (1600–1649; reigned 1625–1649; DNB). It was reported that ‘the left eye [of the corpse], in the first moment of exposure, was open and full, though it vanished almost immediately’, see Sir Henry Halford (1766–1844; DNB), An Account of What Appeared on Opening the Coffin of King Charles the First, in the Vault of King Henry the Eighth in St George’s Chapel at Windsor (1813), p. 8.
– No poet ever imagined any thing more aweful!

RS.
