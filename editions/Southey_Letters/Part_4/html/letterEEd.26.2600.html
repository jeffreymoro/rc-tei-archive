2600. Robert Southey to Henry Herbert Southey, 20 May 1815

(1)
Address: To/ Dr Southey/ Queen Anne Street/ Cavendish Square/ LondonStamped: [partial] WOR/ Unpaid/ Charing CrossPostmark: 10 o’Clock/ 24 MY/ 1815 FNnMS: Bodleian Library, Don. d. 3Unpublished.

Send Wm

(1)
William was a servant in Henry Herbert Southey’s household.
with the inclosed, which is on your business.
(2)
Henry Herbert Southey was standing for election to the post of Senior Physician at the Middlesex Hospital.
I have written to Wynn for Lady Carysforts
(3)
Elizabeth Grenville (1756–1842), Wynn’s aunt and sister of Lord Grenville. She was married to John Joshua Proby, 1st Earl of Carysfort, Irish politician and writer, who had taken some interest in Southey’s early work.
vote, & to Dr Bell for Mr Pike Watts:
(4)
David Pike Watts (1754–1816), a wealthy brewer and vintner.
you will take care of the Bishop
(5)
Shute Barrington (1734–1826; DNB), Bishop of Durham 1791–1826.
yourself, – however as these men like to have a favour asked by as many persons as possible when they mean to grant it, I put him & Sir T. Bernard
(6)
Sir Thomas Bernard (1750–1818; DNB), lawyer and philanthropist.
under the Drs care. Mr Christian in the Strand,
(7)
Henry Curwen Christian (1780–1846), a London merchant from an old Cumberland family. His grandfather, Thomas Christian (1696/7–1770), had been Vicar at Crosthwaite, Keswick.
poor Mrs Wilson here will bespeak for you. I have written to Lord Calthorpe,
(8)
George Gough-Calthorpe, 3rd Baron Calthorpe (1787–1851).
to the Bp of Chester
(9)
George Law (1761–1845; DNB), Bishop of Chester 1812–1824, Bishop of Bath and Wells 1824–1845.
& Wilberforce & shall trouble old Lord Sunderlin & Sir T Ackland.
(10)
Sir Thomas Dyke Acland, 10th Baronet (1787–1871; DNB), politician and philanthropist.
Kindersley
(11)
Nathaniel Edward Kindersley (1763–1831), author of Specimens of Hindoo Literature (1794).
is Mrs Martins cousin,
(12)
Frances Julia Martin (1776–1854), the wife of Thomas Martin (1769–1850), Liverpool merchant and Secretary to the Royal Liverpool Institution. Kindersley was her first cousin.
– I will give her a letter, & then I shall have done my possible. Courtenay & his mother
(13)
Thomas Peregrine Courtenay (1782–1841; DNB), MP for Totnes 1811–1832 and Secretary of the Board of Control 1812–1828. His mother was Lady Elizabeth Howard (d. 1815), daughter of Thomas Howard, 2nd Earl of Effingham (1714–1763).
are on the list, Bedford will speak there for you.

Madoc

(14)
Madoc (1805). A fourth edition was printed in 1815.
is going to press again, & Roderick
(15)
Roderick, the Last of the Goths (1814). A third edition was printed in 1815.
travels so fast that they begin to print a third edition forthwith. The second was 1500 copies, of which 527 only are left. If this fair weather were to continue a little while it would bring me int into deep water. They print 2000 of the third.

Atra Nox

(16)
John William Knox (1784–1862), an usher at Westminster School 1806–1821, clergyman and Latin scholar.
can get Col Drinkwater
(17)
Lieutenant-Colonel John Drinkwater (1762–1844; DNB), military historian and comptroller of army accounts 1811–1836.
for you, if it be the right Bibber of the pure element.

RS.

20 May 1815.
