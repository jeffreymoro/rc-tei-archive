2292. Robert Southey to Henry Herbert Southey, 21 August 1813

(1)
Address: To/ Dr Southey,/ 28. Queen Anne Street, West,/ LondonStamped: KESWICK/ 298Postmark: E/ 24 AU 24/ 1813MS: Bodleian Library, MS Don. d. 3Unpublished.
Saturday – Aug 21. 1813.
My dear Harry

I am weary of waiting, & therefore in xxx expectation that the lump on my eye lid will disperse in the meantime, I have taken my place in Thursdays mail, the coach from here to Penrith serving on Wednesday. So, Deo volente,

(1)
i.e. ‘God willing’.
I shall breakfast with your Lordship on this day week. Will you Let my Uncle know this by twopenny post, & you will save him ten pence, at the cost of only the paper to yourself.

I am “at the receipt of custom”

(2)
Matthew 9: 9.
– this being the full Laking season. This evening, Professor Monk
(3)
James Henry Monk (1784–1856; DNB), who had been elected Regius Professor of Greek at the University of Cambridge in 1809. His travelling companion is unidentified.
came here with some travelling companion.

RS.
