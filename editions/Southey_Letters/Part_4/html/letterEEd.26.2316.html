2316. Robert Southey to John Rickman, [21 October 1813]

Endorsement: RS. – / Augt 1813./ Poeta laureatum
MS: Huntington Library, RS 213
Unpublished.
Dating note: dating from content; Southey learned from Croker about his formal appointment as Poet Laureate on 20 October 1813. This letter was written the following day.

My dear R.

I have looked for you twice without success. My days are indeed numbered in every sense of the word. On Tuesday the 2d. I go with my Uncle & Harry to Woburn,

(1)
Probably to meet John Russell, 6th Duke of Bedford (1766–1839; DNB), the patron of Herbert Hill’s living at Streatham.
& return on Thursday. – On that Thursday si placet,
(2)
‘If it pleases’.
I will come to you, for the few days preceding my departure, which is at present mentally fixed for Tuesday 9th November.

Yesterday, I received official information of my appointment: – & if you go to the next levee, we shall meet in bag & swords!

(3)
Both Rickman and Southey were required to wear the dress appropriate to their offices when they attended Court.

God bless you
RS.
