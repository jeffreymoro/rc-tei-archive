2273. Robert Southey to Grosvenor Charles Bedford, 27 June 1813

(1)
Address: To/ G. C. Bedford Esqr/ ExchequerEndorsement: June 27. 1813MS: Bodleian Library, MS Eng. Lett. c. 25Unpublished.
Sunday June 27. 1813
Dear Grosvenor

The half bills are safe.

(1)
i.e. a half-banknote – a secure way of sending money in the post, by tearing banknotes in half and sending the two halves separately.
– I am just returned from the funeral of my brother in law. –
(2)
George Fricker had died on 24 June 1813; see Robert Southey to Charles Danvers, 28 June 1813, Letter 2274.

Do not buy the stock

(3)
Bedford had been commissioned by Southey to buy government stock on his behalf; see Southey to Grosvenor Charles Bedford, 7 April 1813, Letter 2240.
till you see me, lest peradventure I should want the money when the next pension
(4)
Southey had received a government pension of £200 p.a. (£144 after tax) since 1807.
x replaces what you have now sent.

RS.
