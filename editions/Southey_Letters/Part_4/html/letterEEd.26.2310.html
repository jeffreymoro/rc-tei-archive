2310. Robert Southey to Grosvenor Charles Bedford, [7 October 1813]

(1)
Address: To/ G. C. Bedford Esqre/ ExchequerPostmark: 7 o’Clock/ OC 8/1813 NTEndorsement: 1813MS: Bodleian Library, MS Eng. Lett. c. 25Unpublished.Dating note: Thursday was 7 October in 1813.
My dear Grosvenor

This is ugly weather for locomotion, – but Jove cœterisque xxxxx xxxx faventibus

(1)
‘If Jove favours our meeting.’
we shall be glad to see you tomorrow at your own time & Henry with you if he can come. I am sorry to say there is no bed to offer you here, Mrs Hill expecting two sisters
(2)
Elizabeth Heathcote (1773–1855) and Alethea Bigg (1777–1847).
tomorrow who have already by anticipation turned me out of mine. But there are good beds at the White Lion hard by.

vale
RS.

Streatham.Friday Thursday evening.
